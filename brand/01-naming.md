# Naming Strategy & Recommendation
**Status:** ✓ DECIDED — Arca | 2026-03-18
**Constraint:** propra.ai is owned by a competing proptech company — same sector, domain conflict creates SEO cannibalization and customer confusion risk.

---

## The Naming Brief

The name must carry:
- **Action + intelligence** — this platform doesn't report, it executes
- **Trust + precision** — commission-only, skin in the game; the brand must feel as reliable as the model
- **Premium without pretension** — owner-operators with £5M–£50M portfolios, not Fortune 500 REITs
- **Bilingual market legibility** — FL and SE UK without cultural misfires
- **Domain availability** — preferably .com or .ai; no competitor adjacency in CRE

---

## Naming Shortlist

### 1. ARCA
**Meaning:** Latin *arca* — a strongbox, the chest that holds what is most valuable. Also contains "arc" — trajectory, forward motion.
**Domain options:** `arca.ai` (likely clear), `getarca.com`, `arca.io`
**Why it works:** Four letters. Impossible to misspell. The strongbox metaphor runs all the way through — protecting income, locking in savings, holding the portfolio together. Visual identity anchors on the arc/arch: structural, load-bearing, elegant.
**Risk:** arca.com may be registered (check). Pronunciation clear in both EN-US and EN-GB.

---

### 2. CAIRN
**Meaning:** A stacked stone navigation marker. Used in mountaineering and hiking to mark the right path forward in difficult terrain.
**Domain options:** `cairn.ai` (likely clear), `cairnhq.com`, `getcairn.com`
**Why it works:** Stacked stones = stacked assets. Navigation marker = finding the path through a complex portfolio. Strong UK cultural resonance; understood in the US. Logo writes itself: abstract stacked mark doubles as a bar chart. "Cairn shows you the path forward."
**Risk:** Cairn Energy (UK listed company) holds brand equity in the UK energy space — monitor for confusion. cairn.com taken by a subscription box company.

---

### 3. LUCRA
**Meaning:** From Latin *lucrum* — profit, gain, financial return. The root of "lucrative."
**Domain options:** `lucra.ai` (likely clear), `getlucra.com`, `lucrahq.com`
**Why it works:** Signals the outcome directly — you use Lucra, you make more money. Unusually honest brand name for a B2B SaaS product. "Lucra found £312k sitting in your portfolio." Crisp, memorable.
**Risk:** Lucra is a US sports-betting app (lucra.com taken). Domain workaround required; check for trademark proximity.

---

### 4. VERID
**Meaning:** Contraction of *veridical* (Latin: truth-telling, accurate, verified). Also echoes "verified" and "merit."
**Domain options:** `verid.ai` (likely clear), `verid.com` (check), `getverid.com`
**Why it works:** Positions the platform as the source of truth in a landscape of guesswork and spreadsheets. "Verid verifies every cost, every rent, every decision." Strong differentiator against legacy software that reports but doesn't verify.
**Risk:** Less intuitive meaning on first encounter — requires explanation.

---

### 5. PORTIVO
**Meaning:** Coined from *portfolio* + *active/motive*. Suggests portfolio intelligence in motion.
**Domain options:** `portivo.com` (likely clear), `portivo.ai`
**Why it works:** The product meaning is immediately legible to the target buyer — they manage portfolios, they want them active. No risk of competitor confusion.
**Risk:** Slightly functional; lacks the depth or mystique of the other candidates. Could read as generic proptech.

---

### 6. LANDIS
**Meaning:** Derived from the Anglo-Norman *land* root — landowner, landed estate. Also a surname with heritage weight.
**Domain options:** `landis.ai` (likely clear), `landishq.com`
**Why it works:** Speaks directly to the identity of the buyer — the landlord, the landed owner-operator. Has the feel of a private wealth firm, not a startup. Strong in SE UK where "landed" carries cultural meaning.
**Risk:** Slightly backward-looking; may not signal AI/intelligence capability clearly enough.

---

### 7. NORA
**Meaning:** Acronym potential (Net Operating Returns Advisor), but works as a standalone name. Clean, memorable, non-threatening.
**Domain options:** `nora.ai` (may be taken — check), `asknora.com`
**Why it works:** Persona-forward brand that makes the AI feel like a trusted advisor, not a dashboard. Works if the "Ask Propra AI" feature becomes the front door of the product.
**Risk:** Gendered name may limit enterprise/institutional perception. nora.ai likely registered.

---

### 8. PROPRA (Domain Pivot — Fallback)
**Keep the name, change the domain.**
**Options:** `propra.com` (check — may be available even if propra.ai is taken), `getpropra.com`, `propra.io`
**Why it works:** Zero rebrand cost. The current name is clean, the team knows it, product copy is built around it.
**Risk:** Same-sector competitor on propra.ai creates SEO split and customer confusion. Ongoing friction. Viable only if propra.com is available AND the other company has no trademark on "Propra" in CRE.

## Recommendation: ARCA

**Primary recommendation: `arca.ai` or `getarca.com`**

### Reasoning

Arca wins on three decisive criteria:

**1. Depth of metaphor.**
The strongbox — protecting income, securing value, holding the portfolio — runs through every feature. Insurance protection, energy savings locked in, income streams captured: Arca holds it all. The arc trajectory reinforces the forward-motion, action-oriented positioning. No other candidate has this range.

**2. Visual identity leverage.**
Four letters. Geometric. The arc/arch shape can be abstracted into a mark that reads as structural strength, movement, and precision. The arch is one of the oldest load-bearing structures in architecture — a fitting symbol for a platform built to carry commercial property portfolios.

**3. Market-neutral.**
Works in Jacksonville and in Kent with zero cultural friction. No competitor adjacency. Clean to pronounce and spell in both markets.

**Domain strategy:**
Register `arca.ai` as primary. Register `getarca.com` and `arcahq.com` as defensive. Redirect all to `arca.ai`. Use `@arca.ai` for email.

### Backup: CAIRN
If Arca.ai is registered and no viable workaround exists, Cairn is the strongest fallback. Navigation metaphor is equally compelling; UK resonance is superior; stacked-stone logo concept is immediately distinctive.
