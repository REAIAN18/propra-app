## The Single Differentiating Truth

Every competing platform in this category is built around the same assumption: the owner-operator's job is to manage information. Arca is built on a different assumption: the owner-operator's job is to make money from property. Everything else follows from that.

**The one-line category break:**
> *"Other platforms report. Arca executes."*

This is not hyperbole. Arca places insurance direct with carriers. It switches energy suppliers. It triggers rent reviews. It tenders contractors. It sources debt. It surfaces acquisitions. The gap between a report that says "you're overpaying on energy" and a platform that switches the supplier is the entire product.

## Competitive Map

### Tier 1: Data & Research Platforms

#### CoStar Group (CoStar, LoopNet, STR)
**What they do:** Market data, listings, research, analytics. The dominant source of CRE market intelligence.
**Who uses them:** Brokers, institutions, large owner-operators with dedicated research teams.
**Price:** £10k–£100k+/year subscription. No commission alignment.
**Their strength:** Depth of market data. Network effects. Dominant in listings.
**Their weakness:** Tells you what the market looks like. Does nothing about your specific portfolio. No execution capability. Subscription regardless of outcome.

**Arca's position:**
> "CoStar is the Bloomberg terminal for CRE — if you're a broker or a fund. Arca is the operator for owner-operators: it takes your actual assets, benchmarks them against the market, finds the gap, and closes it. CoStar tells you what properties are worth. Arca tells you what yours is leaving on the table — then fixes it."

**Sales counter to "we already have CoStar":**
> "CoStar shows you the market. It doesn't show you that your energy bill at Unit 4 is 23% above the rate you could switch to today, or that your lease on Block B comes up in 60 days and you're 18% below ERV. That's Arca."

---

#### Reonomy (now part of Altus Group)
**What they do:** Property data, ownership records, deal prospecting for commercial assets.
**Who uses them:** Brokers, lenders, investors doing deal research.
**Their weakness:** Acquisition-only use case. Zero post-acquisition portfolio operations.

**Arca's position:**
> "Reonomy helps you find the deal. Arca runs the asset after you own it."

---

### Tier 2: Property Management Platforms

#### Yardi Systems
**What they do:** Full-stack enterprise property management — accounting, tenant management, leases, maintenance, reporting.
**Who uses them:** Institutional landlords, large REITs, property management companies with 100+ assets.
**Price:** $25k–$250k+/year. Implementation takes months.
**Their strength:** Comprehensive. Deeply integrated with enterprise finance systems. Trusted by institutions.
**Their weakness:** Built for operations managers, not owner-operators making capital decisions. No intelligence layer. No execution on savings or income. The platform tracks what's happening — it doesn't tell you what should be happening differently. Commission misaligned: they invoice regardless of portfolio performance.

**Arca's position:**
> "Yardi manages the machine. Arca optimises what the machine produces. If you're a REIT with 1,000 units and a 20-person finance team, use Yardi. If you're an owner-operator with 12 assets who wants to know whether you're holding or selling asset 7 and whether your insurance is being ripped off, use Arca."

**Sales counter to "we use Yardi":**
> "Yardi will tell you what you spent last month. Arca will tell you what you overspent and fix it. They're not competing — Yardi is your ledger, Arca is your intelligence and execution layer on top of it."

---

#### MRI Software
**What they do:** Commercial and residential property management, portfolio analytics, investor reporting.
**Who uses them:** Mid-to-large commercial landlords, fund managers, REITs.
**Price:** Enterprise contract. Expensive implementation.
**Their weakness:** Same fundamental gap as Yardi — operations and reporting focus, not outcome optimisation. No commission alignment.

**Arca's position:** Identical to Yardi framing. MRI tracks; Arca optimises.

---

#### Buildium
**What they do:** SMB-focused property management — lease tracking, maintenance, tenant communications, basic financials.
**Who uses them:** Residential and small commercial landlords with fewer than 100 units.
**Price:** ~$55–$460/month flat subscription.
**Their strength:** Easy to use. Affordable. Good for tenant management.
**Their weakness:** Completely passive — logs activity, doesn't drive outcomes. No benchmarking. No execution. No intelligence on costs, rents, or opportunities. No commercial property focus.

**Arca's position:**
> "Buildium tracks the work. Arca finds the money. If your primary need is logging maintenance tickets, Buildium does that. If your primary need is knowing whether your 8 commercial assets are earning what they should — and actually doing something about it — that's Arca."

**Why Buildium users are ideal Arca prospects:** They're actively using something, but it does nothing to optimise their portfolio. The upgrade path is simple and the value delta is enormous.

---

#### Arthur Online / Goodlord (UK-focused)
**What they do:** UK property management platforms, primarily residential, some commercial.
**Their weakness:** Residential-first. No CRE intelligence. No execution capability.

**Arca's position:** "Built for commercial, operated by professionals. Not a residential tool retrofitted."

### Tier 3: Point Solutions (Single-Problem Tools)

#### Lease Management: LeaseQuery, Visual Lease, Prophia
**What they do:** Lease accounting and tracking, primarily for tenants (not landlords).
**Arca's counter:** Arca's Rent Clock is built for the landlord — tracking ERV gaps, upcoming review windows, and lease events that represent income risk or opportunity. It's not lease accounting. It's lease optimisation.

#### Insurance: RCIS, CoverWallet, Novarica tools
**What they do:** Commercial property insurance placement, some comparison tools.
**Arca's counter:** Arca identifies the overpay first, then places direct. The insurance module is one instrument in the full portfolio intelligence picture — not a standalone insurance tool.

#### Energy: Utilitrack, EnergyCAP, Panoramic Power
**What they do:** Energy monitoring and reporting for commercial properties.
**Arca's counter:** Monitoring without switching is a report. Arca benchmarks against live market rates and executes the switch. Average saving: £52,000/year per portfolio.

#### Project Management: Procore, Buildertrend
**What they do:** Construction and project management for contractors and developers.
**Arca's counter:** Procore manages the contractor's workflow. Arca manages the owner-operator's exposure — benchmarking quotes before approval, tracking cost vs budget vs market, connecting project outcomes to asset valuation.

---

### Tier 4: Generic SaaS / AI Proptech

#### "AI-powered" proptech startups
The space is full of platforms promising "AI for CRE" that deliver dashboards, alerts, and reports. The common pattern: an AI layer over data aggregation that surfaces insights but stops short of execution.

**Arca's category break vs generic AI proptech:**

| Generic AI proptech | Arca |
|---|---|
| Surfaces an insight | Surfaces an insight AND executes the fix |
| Charges a subscription regardless of outcome | Commission-only — zero fee until you're ahead |
| Broad market focus (brokers, institutions, managers) | Narrow customer focus (owner-operators, 3–30 assets) |
| Sells to operations teams | Sells to the owner |
| Reports on individual cost categories | Full portfolio G2N view across every value stream |
| Built around the data | Built around the outcome |

## Competitive Battlecards (Sales Use)

### "We already use [X]" responses

**"We use CoStar."**
> "CoStar is your market reference. Arca is your portfolio operator. They answer different questions: CoStar tells you what the market looks like; Arca tells you what your specific assets are leaving on the table and fixes it. Most of our clients use both."

**"We use Yardi / MRI."**
> "Those platforms are your ledger — they're excellent at tracking what's happened. Arca works on what should happen differently: benchmarking your costs against market, executing savings, optimising rents, sourcing debt, running acquisitions. Zero setup fee, commission-only. You only pay when you're ahead."

**"We use Buildium."**
> "Buildium is for tenant and maintenance management. Arca is for portfolio optimisation — it works on the money side: insurance, energy, rents, additional income, hold/sell decisions. Different jobs. Many Buildium users add Arca for the intelligence layer."

**"We use spreadsheets."**
> "Arca replaces about fourteen spreadsheets and the six advisors whose job is to populate them. First portfolio health check is free — we'll show you exactly what your assets are leaving on the table before you commit to anything."

**"We don't need software."**
> "You don't need software. You need £412,000 that's currently sitting uncaptured in your portfolio. Arca is how you get it — and we don't invoice until you do."

## Category Creation Strategy

Rather than competing for existing category share, Arca should own a new category:

**"Portfolio optimisation" or "outcome-aligned property intelligence"**

This framing:
- Makes CoStar/Yardi/Buildium feel like complements, not competitors (removes "but we already have X" objection)
- Positions Arca as the layer that makes all existing tools perform
- Anchors the commission model as a feature of the category, not just a pricing quirk
- Gives journalists and analysts a clean hook: *"the first commission-only CRE platform"*

**Suggested category language for PR and investor decks:**
> "Arca created the outcome-aligned property intelligence category — where the platform only earns when the owner does."
