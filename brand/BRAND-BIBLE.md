## 2. What RealHQ Is (One Sentence Each)

**For headlines:** *"Most property software tells you what's wrong. RealHQ fixes it."*

**For elevator pitches:** *"RealHQ benchmarks every cost and income line in a commercial portfolio against live market data, finds where the owner is leaving money on the table, and goes and fixes it. Commission-only — no fee until you're ahead."*

**For press / investor boilerplate:**
> RealHQ is a commission-only commercial real estate intelligence platform for owner-operators with 3–30 assets. It benchmarks every cost and income line against live market data, then executes the fix — insurance placement, energy switching, rent review, ancillary income activation, acquisitions sourcing — taking a fee only on results delivered. RealHQ operates across Florida and South-East UK.

**Category claim:** *"The only CRE platform that combines portfolio intelligence with direct execution, aligned on outcomes rather than subscriptions."*

## 3. Voice — The Hard Rules

### Always
- **Direct.** Say what happens. "RealHQ places it direct." Not "we can help you explore placement options."
- **Specific.** Numbers over adjectives. "£18,000 saving" not "significant savings." "42 days until break clause" not "upcoming lease event."
- **Confident.** No question marks in hero headlines. No "would you like to" in CTAs. RealHQ tells — it doesn't ask.
- **Active.** Subject is RealHQ or the asset. Never passive voice.
- **Peer-level.** Readers are experienced investors who know their numbers. Don't define cap rates. Don't add unnecessary caveats.

### Never
| Never say | Say instead |
|---|---|
| "Our AI-powered platform leverages machine learning to…" | "RealHQ finds the gap. Then closes it." |
| "You may be able to save on insurance." | "You're overpaying £14,200. RealHQ places it direct." |
| "We offer a comprehensive suite of services…" | "Every asset. Every cost. One screen." |
| "Smart," "seamless," "end-to-end" | Specific feature + specific outcome |
| "Would you like to learn more?" | "See your portfolio." |
| "Get started" / "Sign up for free" | "See your portfolio." / "Get your portfolio health check." |
| "Our team reviewed…" / "We called…" | RealHQ found / RealHQ identified / RealHQ placed |
| "We try to…" / "We aim to…" | Either it does it or it doesn't. State the outcome. |
| Lead with AI | Lead with the money. AI is invisible infrastructure. |

**Key rule on automation language:** Never write "our team," "we called," "our advisors," or any phrase that implies human manual work. RealHQ is a platform — it finds, places, switches, flags, and executes. Not "our team found" — "RealHQ found."

## 5. Numbers and Currency

- Always state currency: £ for UK content, $ for US content. Never mix in a single document.
- Always state the period: "£52,000/year" not "£52,000."
- Round to nearest £1,000 in headlines; full precision in tables.
- State the basis where credibility matters: "vs direct market rate" / "vs ERV."
- Large figures set in Instrument Serif typeface.
- **No hardcoded illustrative numbers in UI.** Use live data or generic descriptors (e.g. "—" or "Loading"). Never render demo figures as if they were real.

## 6. Colour — Non-Negotiable Rules

| Name | Hex | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Vault (Dark Navy) | `#0B1622` | Primary background |
| Gain (Green) | `#0A8A4C` | Positive: savings, income, above-benchmark — POSITIVE ONLY |
| Signal (Amber) | `#F5A94A` | Warnings, urgency, risk — NOT for success states |
| Decision (Blue) | `#1647E8` | Interactive elements, links, acquisitions module |

**Never use red** — not in the palette. Gain is positive only. Signal is urgency only. Do not swap them.

Dark-first interface: Vault is always primary background for the core product UI.

---

## 7. Typography — The Rules That Matter

- **Instrument Serif** — hero headlines, large numbers, portfolio totals. Never at body size (below 20px).
- **Geist** — all body copy, labels, UI. Never for hero headlines.
- **Geist Mono** — numbers in tables, figures, IDs.
- Large financial figures always set in Instrument Serif — this is the single most important typographic rule.

## 9. Market-Specific Tone

### Florida (FL)
- Lead with dollars and numbers: G2N benchmarks, dollar savings, IRR
- Emphasise speed: "Live in 48 hours, first saving within 30 days"
- Stress independence: "No advisors. No middlemen."
- Highlight insurance placement as critical (hurricane/FEMA risk)

### South-East UK
- Lead with rent: ERV drift is the most painful, most familiar problem
- Institutional comparison: "The intelligence of a £500M fund, for your 8-asset portfolio"
- Planning Intelligence is highly differentiated — mention it
- Slightly more understated tone; let the numbers speak

## 11. What Not to Show / Write

| Don't | Why |
|---|---|
| Dashboard screenshot in homepage hero | Leads with software, not outcomes |
| Hardcoded demo numbers in UI | Must reflect live data only (Spec Compliance Rule 3) |
| "Our team reviewed your portfolio" | Implies human manual work — RealHQ is the actor |
| Red anywhere | Not in palette; contradicts outcomes-first positioning |
| "Smart," "seamless," "end-to-end" | Generic proptech language. Banned. |
| AI / machine learning in hero copy | Buyers want money, not technology |
| Residential property imagery | This is commercial CRE only |
| All-caps "REALHQ" or "Real HQ" with a space | Name is RealHQ — always |
