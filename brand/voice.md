# Arca Brand Voice
**Version:** 1.0 | 2026-03-19

---

## Three words that describe Arca's voice

**Direct.** We say what we mean. No hedging, no "you may find," no "we try to." Arca states. Arca acts.

**Specific.** Numbers over adjectives. "£18,000 saving" not "significant savings." "42 days until break clause" not "upcoming lease event."

**Confident.** We know this works. We don't pitch — we show. No question marks in hero copy. No "would you like to" in CTAs.

---

## Three words that do not describe Arca's voice

**Fluffy.** We never reach for vague industry language to sound impressive. "Holistic approach," "end-to-end solution," "seamless experience" — these are not us.

**Apologetic.** We never soften our claims with uncertainty. "We try to," "you may be able to," "potentially" — this is not how confident operators speak.

**AI-forward.** We don't lead with the technology. Buyers want money, not machine learning. The AI is in the background. The outcome is in the headline.

## What Arca never says

| Never | Because |
|---|---|
| "Our AI-powered platform leverages machine learning to…" | Leads with technology, not outcome. Buyers want money. |
| "You may be able to save on insurance." | Vague and apologetic. We know the number. State it. |
| "We offer a comprehensive suite of services…" | Generic SaaS language. Specificity is the brand. |
| "Would you like to learn more?" | Arca doesn't ask — it shows. |
| "Get started" / "Sign up for free" | Startup boilerplate. We don't sound like everyone else. |
| "Smart," "seamless," "end-to-end" | Banned. Every proptech says this. |
| "We try to…" / "We aim to…" | Apologetic. Either we do it or we don't. |
| "ARCA" (all caps) | Reads as an acronym. The name is Arca. |

## What Arca does say

| Do | Because |
|---|---|
| "Arca finds the gap. Then closes it." | Subject is Arca. Action is clear. Result is implied. |
| "£18,200 overpay. Arca places it direct." | Number first. Action second. No broker mentioned. |
| "No invoice until you're ahead." | Confident, specific, unconditional. |
| "See your portfolio." | Direct CTA. Arca tells — doesn't ask. |
| "Fix this." | One action. One click. Done. |
| Numbers with currency + period: "£52,000/year" | Always state the currency, always state the period. |

## Voice in different contexts

**Hero headline:** Active, implied verb or declarative. Present tense. No question marks.
> "Every asset earning what it should."

**Feature description:** Lead with what Arca does, then the outcome. One sentence where possible.
> "Arca places your insurance direct with carriers. Average saving: £18,000."

**CTA:** One verb. Direct. Specific to the action.
> "See your portfolio." / "Place direct." / "Run the numbers."

**Sales email:** Specific asset type, specific pain point, specific number. Never generic. Never fluffy. First person — Ian runs Arca, not "our team."

**Error / edge state:** Honest. No corporate apology. "Nothing found" not "We were unable to identify opportunities at this time."

---

*The voice is the product. Every word is a signal of how we operate — direct, specific, outcome-first.*
