5 Ways to Reduce Energy Costs

Optimisation Strategies — Florida Regulated

Savings are cumulative where applicable
1
Tariff restructuring
FPL has 15+ commercial tariff schedules. Most owners are on the wrong one. Moving to a demand-based or time-of-use tariff saves 10–20% with the same supplier, no contract change needed.
RealHQ reviews all FPL/Duke/TECO tariffs for your assets
10–20% of bill
2
Solar PPA
Florida has exceptional solar irradiance. A Power Purchase Agreement means zero upfront cost, a fixed rate below your utility tariff, and an immediate monthly saving from day one.
RealHQ models a PPA for each roof at current FL irradiance
$42k+/yr portfolio
3
Demand charge reduction
40–60% of Florida commercial bills are demand charges, not consumption. Identifying and reducing peak demand events through HVAC scheduling or battery storage cuts the bill significantly.
What RealHQ does
Analyses your bill to isolate demand charges from consumption charges
Identifies peak demand events (time of day, day of week, seasonal patterns)
Recommends HVAC scheduling, load staggering, or battery storage
Calculates ROI for each intervention
Upload bills for RealHQ to identify peak demand events
Up to 25% of demand charges
4
LED and HVAC retrofit
Lighting and HVAC account for 70%+ of commercial energy use. LED and controls upgrades reduce consumption 20–40% and qualify for FPL, Duke, and Tampa Electric rebates.
What RealHQ does
Commissions a free energy audit from a certified assessor — no cost to you
Identifies all applicable utility rebates for your assets
Presents a payback model — typical ROI under 3 years before rebates
You approve. RealHQ manages the installation and rebate claim.
Free · no commitment until you approve the works
20–40% consumption
5
Utility rebate programmes
FPL, Duke Energy Florida, and Tampa Electric all run commercial efficiency rebate programmes worth $50k–$200k per site. Most commercial owners never claim them.
RealHQ identifies all rebates available for your assets
$50k+ per site
