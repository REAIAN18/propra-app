## Insurance Benchmarks (Florida — Hurricane/Wind Zone)

Florida insurance is the single biggest portfolio cost line that owners systematically underestimate and under-market-test. Premiums have risen 30-60% since 2021 (Citizens Property Insurance crisis; private market retreats).

| Asset Type | Market Rate (All-Risk + Wind) | Legacy Over-Contract | Typical Overpay |
|---|---|---|---|
| Office (concrete, mid-rise) | $1.80–$2.80/sqft | $2.50–$4.00/sqft | 20–35% |
| Retail (inline NNN) | $1.80–$2.50/sqft | $2.50–$4.00/sqft | 25–40% |
| Industrial / Warehouse (tilt-up) | $0.80–$1.40/sqft | $1.20–$2.00/sqft | 20–35% |
| Flex (light industrial/office mix) | $1.40–$2.20/sqft | $2.00–$3.20/sqft | 25–40% |

**Why FL insurance is always worth auditing:**
1. The market has been in chaos since 2021 — many owners on auto-renewed policies that pre-date the current market
2. New carriers entered 2024-2025 offering more competitive rates for newer builds with impact windows and metal roofs
3. Many small-medium owners placed insurance through a single broker who has not re-marketed the risk in years
4. Wind mitigation credits (roof shape, opening protection, roof-to-wall attachment) are frequently under-utilised

**RealHQ demo portfolio FL insurance overpay:**
- fl-001 Coral Gables Office: $112k vs $84k market = **$28k/yr overpay** (33%)
- fl-002 Brickell Retail: $34k vs $22.5k market = **$11.5k/yr overpay** (51%)
- fl-003 Tampa Industrial: $42k vs $28k market = **$14k/yr overpay** (50%)
- fl-004 Orlando Office: $86k vs $62k market = **$24k/yr overpay** (39%)
- fl-005 Fort Lauderdale Flex: $54k vs $38k market = **$16k/yr overpay** (42%)
- **Total: ~$93.5k/yr recoverable on insurance alone**

## Energy Benchmarks (Florida)

> **Important:** Florida has regulated monopoly utilities (FPL, Duke Energy Florida, TECO). There is **no competitive retail electricity market** — commercial customers cannot switch electricity suppliers. The energy saving mechanism for FL is **rate schedule optimization** (moving to the optimal FPL/TECO tariff for the asset's consumption and demand profile), demand charge management, and solar PV — not supplier switching.

| Utility | Optimal Rate Schedule (effective blended rate) | Default Rate (GS-2 equivalent) | Overpay from wrong schedule |
|---|---|---|---|
| FPL (South FL) | $0.12–$0.15/kWh (on optimal schedule) | $0.15–$0.20/kWh (GS-2 default) | 10–25% |
| Duke Energy (Central FL) | $0.11–$0.14/kWh (on optimal schedule) | $0.14–$0.18/kWh (default tariff) | 12–25% |
| TECO (Tampa) | $0.11–$0.14/kWh (on optimal schedule) | $0.14–$0.18/kWh (default tariff) | 15–28% |

**Key facts:**
- FL benefits from low generation costs relative to the Northeast — baseline rate is favourable
- FPL has 14+ commercial rate schedules (GS-1, GS-2, GSLD, LP-2, SL-2, etc.). Most assets are on GS-2 default at meter setup and never reassessed. Rate schedule audits typically find 10–22% savings on portfolios where the schedule hasn't been reviewed since first occupancy.
- Demand charges ($/kW of peak demand) are 35–55% of FL commercial electricity bills. Demand response programs, battery storage, and operational scheduling can reduce these by 15–30%.
- Solar PV makes exceptional economic sense in FL: 5.0–5.5 peak sun hours/day (vs 3.5–4.0 in the Northeast). PACE financing eliminates upfront cost. Payback period 5–8 years.
- **In demo calls:** Frame as "rate schedule audit + demand optimisation" not "energy switching." FL prospects know their utility is a regulated monopoly — "switching" language will immediately flag as UK/deregulated market framing.

**Typical energy consumption by building type (FL climate):**
- Office (ventilated, multi-story): 18-28 kWh/sqft/year — driven by AC load in summer
- Retail (incl. tenant equipment): 20-35 kWh/sqft/year — food/bev tenants are highest
- Industrial/warehouse (minimal climate control): 6-12 kWh/sqft/year
- Flex: 12-20 kWh/sqft/year
