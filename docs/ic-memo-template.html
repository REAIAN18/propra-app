Executive Summary

This memorandum presents the investment case for the acquisition of Regency House, a 30,150 sq ft multi-let office building in Basildon, Essex. The property is currently 100% vacant following completion of a £5.3m refurbishment programme in 2022. This opportunity represents a leasing execution play, not a refurbishment or development opportunity.

Investment Thesis

Value creation is predicated on successful letting of the vacant space at market rents (£22-24 psf effective) within an 18-24 month timeframe. The asset benefits from recent capital investment (Cat A specification throughout) but faces material leasing risk given secondary market dynamics: 18% vacancy rate, falling take-up (-23% YoY), and 95,000 sq ft of competing supply within a 1-mile radius.

UK Office Market Overview

The UK office sector continues to face structural demand challenges driven by hybrid working adoption (estimated 35-40% of pre-pandemic office attendance in secondary markets). Key trends:

  • Flight to quality: Tenant demand concentrated in Grade A stock with ESG credentials. Secondary assets face prolonged void periods and increased incentive packages (12-18 months rent-free typical vs 6-9 months pre-2020).
  • Yield expansion: Secondary office yields out 150-200 bps since 2021 (now 8-10% vs 6.5-8% pre-pandemic). Prime-secondary spread widening.
  • Regional divergence: London and core regional cities (Manchester, Birmingham, Edinburgh) outperforming. Peripheral markets (e.g. Basildon, Slough, Reading) experiencing material vacancy increases.

EXIT RISK ASSESSMENT

Key Risks to Exit Execution:

  • Buyer pool constraint: Multi-let secondary offices in peripheral locations have limited buyer depth. Typical buyers are regional/local investors with extended due diligence periods (12-16 weeks typical).
  • Liquidity discount: Forced sale or time-constrained exit could require 15-25% discount to market value.
  • Tenant concentration risk: Single tenant >40% of income creates buyer concern. Diversification across 4-6 tenants essential.
  • Covenant quality: Institutional buyers require minimum 40% of income from investment-grade tenants. SME-heavy tenant base limits buyer universe.

Mitigation: (i) Target 18-24 month pre-marketing window, (ii) ensure stabilised income >12 months pre-sale, (iii) maintain WAULT >4 years at exit, (iv) engage multiple agents to maximise buyer reach.
