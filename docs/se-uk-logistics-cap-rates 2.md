## Net Initial Yield (NIY) Benchmarks — SE UK Logistics

In UK commercial real estate, the **Net Initial Yield** (cap rate equivalent) = Net Passing Income ÷ Purchase Price (inclusive of acquisition costs, or exclusive depending on convention — note: UK convention is typically quoted exclusive of stamp duty and transaction costs).

### Prime Logistics (M25 / Core SE England)

| Specification | NIY Range | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Prime M25 big-box (>150k sqft), Grade A, 10+ yr lease, institutional covenant | 4.25–4.75% | Amazon, Ocado, Royal Mail quality covenants |
| Prime M25 mid-box (50k–150k sqft), Grade A/B, 7–10 yr lease, strong covenant | 4.50–5.00% | DHL, XPO, Wincanton |
| M25 fringe / Gatwick corridor, modern, 5–7 yr lease | 5.00–5.50% | Good access, not prime M25 proximity |
| **Short WAULT (<3 years), reversionary pricing** | **5.50–6.50%** | **Near-vacant or expiring; priced on ERV, not passing rent** |

## NOI / G2N Benchmarks — SE UK Logistics

**G2N = Net Operating Income ÷ Gross Income**

| Lease Structure | G2N Benchmark | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| FRI (Full Repairing & Insuring), single tenant | 80–88% | Tenant pays all outgoings; landlord only insurance and management |
| FRI with service charge (multi-let) | 72–80% | Landlord manages common areas; service charge partially recoverable |
| Multi-let, older estate, higher void provision | 65–74% | Void rates, higher management, EPC capex provision |
| Short lease / high vacancy | 58–68% | Void-related costs drag on NOI |

**Typical NOI deductions for a SE UK logistics portfolio:**

| Cost Line | FRI Single-Let (% of GRI) | Multi-Let (% of GRI) |
|---|---|---|
| Buildings insurance (landlord) | 2–4% | 3–5% |
| Property management fee | 1–2% | 2–4% |
| Void costs (rates, services) | 0–3% | 3–8% |
| Letting / lease renewal costs | 0.5–1.5% | 1–3% |
| Planned maintenance / sinking fund | 1–3% | 2–4% |
| Ground rent / head lease (if applicable) | 0–2% | 0–2% |
| **Total deductions** | **4–15%** | **11–26%** |
| **Implied G2N** | **85–96%** | **74–89%** |

**Note on SE UK data:** FRI logistics G2N of 85%+ is achievable for well-let assets with long leases. The 74% portfolio benchmark in RealHQ's demo data reflects a portfolio with some multi-let exposure, older stock, and moderate management costs — this is credible for a mixed SE logistics portfolio.

## WAULT Analysis

**WAULT = Weighted Average Unexpired Lease Term** — the single most important valuation driver after location and spec.

| WAULT | Valuation Impact | Typical Investor |
|---|---|---|
| 10+ years | Premium pricing; NIY at low end of range | Core / Core+ funds; REITs |
| 7–10 years | Mid-market pricing; institutional-grade | Balanced funds; value-add |
| 4–7 years | Modest discount; reversionary upside story | Value-add; opportunistic |
| 2–4 years | Material discount; priced for vacancy risk | Value-add; owner-occupier |
| <2 years | Significant discount; valued on ERV or VP | Opportunistic; developers |

**Break clauses:**
A break clause within 12 months that has not been resolved is treated by the market as effectively equivalent to a lease expiry. A 7-year lease with a 12-month unresolved break is priced as a 1-year lease with a 7-year reversionary.

## NOI Calculation — SE UK Logistics (Per-Asset)

### How to calculate NOI for UK logistics:

```
Gross Rent Roll
  − Void (empty units at market rent × void rate)
  − Irrecoverable costs (insurance, management, void rates)
  + Ancillary income (solar, EV charging, mast income)
= Net Operating Income (NOI)
```

**Key UK-specific items:**
- **Business rates relief during void:** Landlords pay 100% of empty property rates after the first 3 months free period (6 months for industrial). This is a significant cost for vacant logistics assets.
- **Empty rates on SE UK logistics (2023 revaluation):** Approximately £4.50–£7.50/sqft rateable value for SE logistics, meaning a 68k sqft vacant building at £6/sqft RV costs ~£408k/yr in rates. This is the 'hidden cost' of a Gravesend-type vacancy.
- **Incentive packages on re-letting:** 12–18 months rent-free is typical for SE logistics in current market. Capitalised incentive cost of £1–2M on a 68k sqft building must be deducted from reversionary value.
