## Post 5 — Rent Roll Problem (publish Day 7)

Here's a thing that costs commercial landlords more money than almost anything else:

Letting a rent review pass without preparing for it.

Here's why it matters:

Most leases have upward-only rent review clauses, typically every 3–5 years. At review date, you submit a proposal. The tenant (or their surveyor) counters. You negotiate.

The negotiation outcome depends almost entirely on how well you've mapped comparable transactions in the preceding 6–12 months.

Most owner-operators don't track comps. Their lease hits review date, they ask their solicitor, the solicitor quotes a rate based on whatever's to hand, and they settle for less than the market would support.

On a 10-asset portfolio, the cumulative cost of under-prepared rent reviews over one lease cycle can easily be £40k–£80k in foregone income.

Arca tracks live ERV for every lease in the portfolio. We build the comps case before review date, not at it.

This is one of five things we do. Commission-only. No cost if we don't deliver the uplift.
