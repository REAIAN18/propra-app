## The Problem Frame (2:00–5:00)

"Most logistics owner-operators I talk to have the same three blind spots.

First: energy. Industrial and logistics assets use a lot of electricity — fork lifts, lighting, loading bay systems. Most of that spend is on commercial tariffs that haven't been touched since before Ofgem's supply chain repricing in 2022–23. The market has moved materially, but the contracts haven't.

Second: insurance. Commercial property policies on industrial estates tend to run on the same schedule for years. They're not portfolio-rated, they're not retendered, and the carrier knows that. The result is consistent overpayment — usually 25–40% above what a properly structured portfolio placement would cost.

Third: the rooftops. Every logistics shed you own is a solar revenue opportunity sitting dormant. Add large-vehicle EV charging bays and 5G mast sites — which networks are actively acquiring in the SE corridor — and you have a meaningful income stream with zero capex.

Let me show you what this looks like in numbers."

## UK-Specific Context to Have Ready

- **MEES 2025**: From April 2025, it is unlawful to let commercial property below EPC Band E. From 2028, Band C. Logistics owners with older sheds are exposed.
- **Ofgem commercial repricing**: The energy crisis repriced commercial supply chain contracts in 2022–23. Many contracts placed before that are now significantly above market.
- **5G mast demand**: BT/EE, Vodafone, and Three are all expanding SE coverage. Industrial rooftops are prime locations. Typical licence: 10–15 years, £18–28k/year per mast.
- **EV HGV charging**: Government ZEV mandate means fleet operators must electrify by 2035. Large-format charging infrastructure is in acute short supply in SE logistics corridors.
- **Solar PPA**: Zero capex for the owner. Typical SE roof (500–800kWp) yields £75–120k/year in rental income under a 20-year PPA.
