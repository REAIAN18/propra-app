## SE UK Sub-Market Context — Wave 2 Focus Areas

- **Kent:** Post-Ofgem repricing 2022–2023 means most Kent industrial operators still on legacy tariffs. MEES 2027 is approaching and enforcement activity is increasing. Lloyd's specialist insurance market vs. regional Kent brokers = 15–25% premium gap typical. M2/M20/A2 corridor operators have high logistics-sector tenants (energy intensity).
- **Essex:** Largest industrial sub-market in SE UK outside London. Gravesend/Thurrock (proximity to Dartford Crossing, London Gateway) is fastest-growing corridor. Basildon/South Essex A127 corridor is densely held by private family owners. Mid-Essex (Braintree, Chelmsford) has highest concentration of legacy contracts and MEES-exposed pre-2000 stock.
- **Hertfordshire:** A1/M1/M25 junction zone is high-value logistics sub-market. New-build completions are regular — energy procurement at handover is the key overpay point. Older Hemel Hempstead/Watford stock has significant MEES exposure. Insurance on multi-let Herts estates is typically placed with regional brokers unaware of Lloyd's specialist capacity.
- **Bedfordshire:** A1/M1 corridor logistics is growing rapidly (Biggleswade, Bedford, Luton/Dunstable). Spec development with retained ownership creates recurring energy and insurance procurement opportunities. MEES on any pre-2016 Beds stock is commercially sensitive ahead of 2027.
