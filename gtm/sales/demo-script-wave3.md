## PART 2 — Transaction Room (LIVE)

**Navigate to:** `/transactions` OR from asset page (hold/sell section) → "Open transaction room →" OR from Scout → "Open deal room →"

**Opening line:**

> "Once you've decided to buy or sell — or you're in negotiation on a deal from Scout — the Transaction Room is where it all happens. It's not a folder, it's a deal execution environment."

### 2a. For a SALE — show from asset page

**Navigate to:** Asset page → Hold/Sell section → "Open transaction room →"

> "This is a sale room. The seller is you; the buyer is whoever you're in negotiation with. The room tracks the 7 standard milestones from NDA to completion — NDA signed, heads of terms agreed, solicitors instructed, searches ordered, survey instructed, exchange, completion."

**[Screen: Transaction room — milestone tracker]**

> "Each milestone is a tile. When it's complete, you click it, add a date and note, it turns green. Everyone in the deal — you, your solicitor, the buyer's solicitor if you give them access — can see exactly where the deal is. No more 'what's happening with the search?' calls."

### 2b. Document vault

> "The document vault is organised by category — legal, financial, technical, correspondence. You upload once; the documents are here for the life of the deal. NDAs, searches, survey report, draft contracts — it's all in one place, not scattered across email threads."

**[Show document vault section]**

### 2c. NDA workflow

> "When you're ready to share financials, you send an NDA through the platform. The counterparty gets a link, reviews the terms, and confirms online. The signed NDA is automatically added to the document vault. If you have DocuSign connected, it goes through DocuSign — legally executed, timestamped. If not, it's a PDF they confirm receipt of."

### 2d. For a PURCHASE — show from Scout

**Navigate to:** Scout → any deal in pipeline → "Open deal room →"

> "When a deal you've liked in Scout gets serious — you've had the call, you're past the initial screen — you open a deal room. Same structure, but now it's a buy-side room. You're tracking from your first offer to exchange."

**Commission on completion:**

> "When a deal completes through the Transaction Room and an agreed price is entered, we record a commission. 0.25% of the agreed price. On a £5M industrial asset that's £12,500. We earn nothing until the deal closes."

**Qualification question:**
> "Do you have a live deal — buy or sell — where the paperwork is currently in email threads or a Dropbox folder? That's exactly what this replaces."

## PART 3 — Tenant Portal (COMING Sprint 4 — ~6 weeks)

> "Coming in the next sprint: a tenant-facing portal. Your tenants get a magic link — no login to create, no account to manage. They click through and they can see their lease summary, their next rent due date, submit a maintenance request, and message you."

**The hook for FL multi-tenant office/retail:**

> "Think about your Coral Gables office — 8 tenants. Right now, where does tenant communication happen? What's the first thing you get when an AC unit goes down at 9pm on a Thursday? A WhatsApp message. This replaces that. The tenant submits a maintenance request, you get a work order in your dashboard, the contractor gets deployed. All without a phone call."

**Rent payment angle (for UK SE logistics):**

> "For UK landlords, tenants can set up a Direct Debit mandate for rent — you get notified the moment rent clears. No more chasing whether the bank transfer went through. You know by 9am on the 1st of the month whether rent was collected."

**For FL prospects re: payment:**

> "For US tenants, ACH direct payment is in the following sprint — 8–10 weeks out. The portal and maintenance workflow is live before that."

## PART 4 — Legal Document Automation (COMING Sprint 4 — ~6 weeks)

**For UK logistics prospects specifically — do not lead with Section 25 on FL calls.**

**UK angle:**

> "Coming alongside the Tenant Portal: legal document automation. When a tenant's lease is coming up for renewal and they're protected under the Landlord and Tenant Act 1954, you need to serve a formal Section 25 Notice. That's typically £250–£400 for a solicitor to draft and check. The platform drafts it in 60 seconds, pre-populated with your tenant's details, the correct grounds, the prescribed statutory form. You still want your solicitor to review it — we say that clearly in the platform — but the drafting cost goes from £350 to £200 and the turnaround goes from 5 days to 5 minutes."

**FL/US angle:**

> "For US leases, the equivalent is the Licence to Assign — when a tenant wants to transfer their lease to a new occupier. Platform drafts the licence, includes the direct covenant clause, flags whether a guarantor is needed based on the assignee's covenant grade. AI draft: $200. Solicitor review: still needed, but you're starting from a reviewed draft rather than a blank instruction."

**Documents in scope for Sprint 4:**
- Section 25 Notice (LTA 1954 — UK only)
- Licence to Assign (UK + US)
- Licence to Underlet (UK + US)
- Deed of Variation (UK + US)

## Demo Notes

- **Brochure Generator is a showstopper.** Prospects who own assets always respond to seeing their own property profile produced in 60 seconds. Use it with a real asset address if possible — not the demo data.
- **Transaction Room is best demonstrated for prospects with live deals.** Ask in qualification: "Are you in any active negotiations right now, buy or sell?" If yes, the Transaction Room is a genuine tool for them, not a demo feature.
- **Tenant Portal — do not promise FL rent payment in Sprint 4.** ACH for US is Sprint 5. Don't overclaim.
- **Section 25 / Legal docs — UK only for the statutory instruments.** FL prospects: pivot to Licence to Assign and Deed of Variation — these are genuinely useful in Florida commercial leases.
- **Scout → Transaction Room is the smoothest flow for active acquirers.** Show the deal in Scout, click "Open deal room →", show the milestone tracker. 3 minutes end-to-end.
