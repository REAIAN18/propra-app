# Demo Call Script — 20-Minute Portfolio Review
**Version:** 1.0 | **Audience:** FL & SE UK owner-operators, 3–30 assets
**Format:** Screen share + live scan walkthrough at /scan
**Goal:** Book the first paid engagement (insurance audit OR energy review) before the call ends

---

## Pre-Call Setup (5 min before)
- Open /scan with their portfolio pre-loaded (addresses in hand)
- Have their insurance renewal month if known (from outreach reply)
- Know: asset count, rough sector (industrial / mixed-use / retail), state/region

---

## Min 0–2 | Hook

> "Thanks for making time. I pulled your portfolio before this call — [N] assets, [City / region]. I want to show you three numbers in the next 20 minutes. If they're interesting, great. If not, no harm done.
>
> The numbers I'm looking at are: what you're probably overpaying on insurance, what you could be saving on energy, and whether there's any income you're not capturing. Commission-only — I only make money if I find something real and recover it for you.
>
> Let me share my screen."

**[Share screen — open /scan with their portfolio]**

---

## Min 2–8 | Live Scan Walkthrough

Navigate to `/scan`. Walk through each section **using their actual figures, not generic examples**.

**Insurance panel:**

> "This is the insurance benchmark. Based on your asset types and locations, you're carrying roughly [$X] in annual premium. The market benchmark for a comparable portfolio right now is [$Y]. That's a [$Z] gap — [%] above where you could be.
>
> That's not unusual. FL commercial premiums repriced hard after 2022. Most owners I talk to are still on contracts placed in a different market."

**Energy panel:**

> "Energy — [N] of your assets are commercial electricity purchasers. Based on square footage and sector, the modelled gap versus best available tariff is [$X–$Y] a year. That's before we do a proper meter-level audit."

**Additional income panel:**

> "This one's different — it's revenue, not cost. We look at whether any assets have untapped income potential: EV charging, subletting, ancillary. On your portfolio the model flags [outcome] as worth investigating."

**Pause.**

> "Any of these land differently given what you actually know about your contracts?"

*[Listen. Let them push back or confirm. This is the key qualification moment.]*

---

## Min 8–14 | The Model

> "Before I go further — let me be clear on how this works, because people assume there's a catch.
>
> We're commission-only. No retainer, no upfront fee, no invoice until we've delivered a saving or a new income stream. Our commission is a percentage of what we recover — so if we find nothing, you pay nothing. If we find [$40k], we take a slice and you keep the rest.
>
> [Screen: open /pricing]
>
> The way it splits: insurance — we take [X%] of year-one savings. Energy — [X%] of the first 12 months. Additional income — [X%] of the first year's income. It's aligned. We only win when you win."

---

## Min 14–18 | Their Situation

Move off the screen share. Conversation mode.

> "Let me ask you a couple of direct questions."

**Insurance:**
> "When did you last put your commercial insurance out to tender? Not just renew — actually benchmark it against the market?"

*[If 3+ years ago or never:]*
> "That's the norm, honestly. Most owners renew with the same broker because it's easy. But the market has moved — what was competitive in 2021 isn't competitive now. That's where the gap comes from."

**Energy:**
> "Are any of your energy contracts up for renewal in the next 6–12 months?"

*[If yes:]*
> "That's the moment to act. Switching mid-contract has an exit cost — but at renewal you have full leverage. We can run the audit now and have the options ready before you renew."

**Additional income** (only if scan flagged something credible):
> "The [EV charging / subletting] flag on [asset address] — do you know if that's ever been looked at?"

---

## Min 18–20 | Next Step

Don't ask "are you interested." Ask for a specific action.

> "Here's what I'd suggest. The insurance benchmark is the quickest win — I can start the audit this week. All I need is your current policy schedule and the addresses. It takes me about 5 working days to come back with a full market comparison.
>
> If the gap is real, we move forward. If it isn't, you've spent nothing and you know where you stand.
>
> Can you send me the policy schedule and I'll kick it off?"

**If they want to think about it:**
> "No problem. When is your insurance renewal? I'll put a note to follow up a month before — that's when the timing actually matters."

**If they want the energy audit instead:**
> "Same process — I'll need your meter numbers or a recent bill. Which asset do you want to start with?"

---

## Notes for Ian

- **Don't pad the silence.** After showing a number, stop talking. Let them react.
- **If they ask "how accurate are these numbers?"** → "They're model-based — rough benchmarks. The real number comes from the audit. But in my experience, the direction is almost always right. The question is whether the gap is [$10k] or [$40k]."
- **If they push on commission rate:** → "Happy to walk through the exact calc once we have real numbers. The structure doesn't change — you only pay if we deliver."
- **Objection — "we have a broker/energy consultant":** → "Good. We work alongside brokers, not instead of them. We bring market data they don't always have visibility on. If they've already benchmarked you, we'll probably find the same thing. But if they haven't — we find it first."
- **Hard stop at 20 min.** Respect their time. You asked for 20 minutes, hold to it.
