## Post 1 — The FL Rate Schedule Trap (Day -4)
**Angle:** FPL and TECO commercial rate schedules. Most Palm Beach and Broward industrial owners are on whatever rate schedule FPL assigned at meter setup — GS-2 is the default, but it is often not the most cost-effective one for their actual consumption and demand profile. Rate schedule audits consistently find 10-20% savings with no operational change required.

---

Something FL industrial owners rarely check:

Florida Power & Light assigns a commercial rate schedule when your meter goes live. Most of the time it's GS-2 — the standard general service schedule. It works. Nobody revisits it.

FPL has fourteen commercial rate schedules. They're designed for different consumption profiles: different load factors, different peak-to-off-peak ratios, different demand intensities. GS-2 is the right schedule for some portfolios. It's the wrong one for many.

The demand charge — often 40–50% of an industrial or flex asset's electricity bill — is particularly sensitive to which rate schedule you're on. Two buildings with identical annual kWh consumption can have electricity bills that differ by 15–22% based solely on rate schedule.

On a 50,000 sq ft industrial or flex park in Palm Beach County or Broward, that's typically $9k–$18k/year sitting in a rate schedule mismatch. Not because FPL is doing anything wrong — because the rate schedule was never reviewed after first occupancy.

Rate schedule optimisation for a commercial FL asset takes four to six weeks. The saving recurs every year.
