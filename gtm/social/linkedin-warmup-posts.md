## Post 1 — The Problem (Day -3)
**Angle:** FL commercial landlords overpaying on insurance. Hurricane repricing since 2022. Most policies never shopped. Insider knowledge tone.

---

Something FL commercial property owners rarely know:

The insurance market repriced after Hurricane Ian in 2022 — and again after Idalia in 2023. Lloyd's pulled back. Regional carriers exited. The carriers who stayed raised rates 40, 50, 60% in some cases.

Most owners accepted the increase and moved on.

Here's what they didn't do: shop it.

The average FL commercial portfolio I've looked at is paying 28–35% above what direct-placed coverage costs today. On a $200k annual premium, that's $56k–$70k a year. Recurring. Every year.

The repricing since 2022 is real — but most of the overpay isn't the market. It's the broker relationship. Brokers earn on placement. They're not incentivised to find you the cheapest option.

Most FL commercial policies haven't been competitively shopped in 3+ years.

When did you last get a competing bid on yours?
