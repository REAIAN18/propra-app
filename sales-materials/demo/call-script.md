# RealHQ — 20-Minute Demo Call Script

**Format:** Screen share of arcahq.ai dashboard (live or staged FL demo portfolio)
**Goal:** Establish credibility → surface their real situation → show the model → agree a portfolio health check

---

## PRE-CALL PREP (5 min before the call)

**Look up:**
- County appraiser / tax records for their known assets (know the asset types, rough size, location)
- Any public CoStar / LoopNet listings — note asking rents, vacancy indicators
- LinkedIn: how long they've owned, any prior sales, whether they're actively acquiring

**Tabs to have open:**
1. arcahq.ai — FL mixed demo portfolio loaded and ready
2. Their county appraiser page (if you found it)
3. Your calendar link (cal.com/realhq/portfolio-review) for rebooking if needed
4. One-pager PDF — ready to paste into chat

**Mental prep:**
- Know their first name and how to say it
- If you know asset types: think about what their likely exposures are (retail strip → insurance/anchor risk; industrial → energy; mixed → all three)
- Set the frame: you're a peer, not a vendor. Founder to founder.

---

## OPENING (0:00–2:00)

> "[First name], thanks for the time. Here's how I want to use the next 20 minutes.
>
> I'm going to show you what RealHQ actually finds on a portfolio — real numbers, not a deck. I'll use a Florida mixed-use demo so you can see the format.
>
> Before I get into it, I want to make sure this is actually relevant to you — so I'll ask you three questions first. Then I'll show you the dashboard. Then if it makes sense, we talk next steps.
>
> Sound fair?"

**[Wait for yes — don't skip this beat. It sets collaborative tone.]**

---

## DISCOVERY (2:00–7:00)

Ask these in order. Listen more than you talk.

---

**Q1: Portfolio makeup**

> "Quick orientation — how many assets are you running, and roughly what mix? Retail, office, industrial, mixed?"

**Listen for:**
- Size (3–30 assets is your sweet spot)
- Asset types (determines which levers matter most — insurance/energy/rent/additional income)
- Whether they have a managing agent (common objection territory — address later)

---

**Q2: The pain question**

> "What's the one thing about running the portfolio that takes the most time or costs you the most money that you feel like you never quite get on top of?"

**Listen for:**
- Insurance frustration ("just rolls every year, I don't bother shopping it")
- Lease admin ("we've got holdovers I keep meaning to formalise")
- Energy ("I know we're paying too much but sorting it feels like a project")
- If they say "nothing, it runs fine" — probe: "Are you actively benchmarking your insurance vs market, or on a managed renewal cycle?"

---

**Q3: Last time they benchmarked**

> "When did you last run a competitive retender on your insurance? Just curious — most people I talk to it's been 3+ years."

**Listen for:**
- "We just renewed" = opportunity: renewal ≠ retender. Address in demo.
- "We have a broker who handles it" = common. Not an objection — your answer is "brokers earn from carriers; we earn from your saving."
- "Last year" = still worth checking. Carrier panel matters as much as timing.

---

**[Pivot to demo:]**
> "Good — let me show you what this looks like in practice. I'm going to screen share."

---

## DEMO WALK (7:00–17:00)

**Share arcahq.ai dashboard. Keep it moving. Don't narrate every pixel.**

---

### Screen 1: G2N Overview (2 min)

> "This is the Gross-to-Net screen. Every asset, income vs costs vs the optimised state.
>
> The amber bar is the delta — what the portfolio is currently leaving on the table versus what live market data says it should be netting. On this demo portfolio that's $41k a year.
>
> Everything here is sourced from live benchmarks — not estimates, not ranges. Actual quotes we can action."

**[Point to the gap. Let the number land. Don't rush.]**

---

### Screen 2: Insurance (3 min)

> "Insurance first — because it's usually the biggest and fastest win.
>
> This portfolio had three assets on renewal-rolled policies. We ran a retender through our carrier panel — Lloyd's, AIG, Chubb, Travelers. New placement: 28% below renewal. That's $22k a year.
>
> The saving is on paper before you sign anything. You see the new quote versus your renewal, you approve, we place it. Takes 3–4 weeks.
>
> We earn 15% of year-1 saving. You keep the rest every year after."

**[If they mentioned a broker in discovery:]** > "Your broker earns commission from the carrier — their incentive is placement, not saving. We earn from the saving — our incentive is to get you the lowest rate."

---

### Screen 3: Energy (2 min)

> "Energy. Two assets on legacy commercial tariffs — never switched since original contract. We benchmarked them per-kWh against live market rates, switched the supplier.
>
> $11k a year saving. 3 weeks to execute. We earn 10% of year-1.
>
> You don't touch this — we handle the switch. You just confirm."

---

### Screen 4: Rent Roll + ERV (2 min)

> "Rent roll mapped against ERV — Estimated Rental Value from comparable transactions in the last 6 months.
>
> Asset 3 is at $62k. Market is $74k. $12k delta. The lease has a rent review clause in 8 months — which means we have lead time to prepare the evidence pack now, not scramble at review date.
>
> Asset 5 is on a holdover tenancy. No formal lease. That's a liability and a missed opportunity — and it's more common than people think."

**[If they flagged lease issues in discovery, name it back:]** > "This is exactly what you mentioned earlier with [their specific pain]. This is how we'd approach it."

---

### Screen 5: Additional Income (1 min)

> "Last screen — income the portfolio isn't currently earning.
>
> EV charging operator interested in the car park on Asset 2. $8k/yr, 10-year contract. 5G mast on Asset 4's roof — comparable sites in the area are at $11–16k/yr.
>
> We only flag what we can actually execute. If we put it on the screen, we have an operator ready to survey."

---

## CLOSE (17:00–20:00)

**Step down through yes → maybe → no.**

---

**The check-in question:**

> "Does any of that look materially different from your situation — or does this feel like it could apply to your portfolio?"

**[Pause. Let them answer. This is the most important moment on the call.]**

---

**If YES / interested:**

> "The fastest way to find out what your portfolio has on the table is a portfolio health check. We need your insurance schedules, energy contracts, and rent roll. Takes 20 minutes to upload — we come back within 5 business days with a real number.
>
> Nothing to sign. If the number isn't worth pursuing, you've lost 20 minutes. If it is, we agree terms and start executing.
>
> Want to set that up? I can send you a secure upload link right now."

---

**If MAYBE / hesitant:**

> "What's the main thing giving you pause?"

**[Listen. Common responses:]**

- *"I need to think about it"* → "Totally fair. What would help you feel more comfortable — would it help to start with just the insurance schedule? That alone usually finds the first saving, no other data needed."
- *"I'm not sure about sharing financials"* → "We can start with insurance only — public rate information, nothing sensitive. That gets you a real data point before you decide to go further."
- *"I have a managing agent"* → "Managing agents don't typically do insurance retenders or energy switching — different lane. We'd work alongside them, not replace them."

---

**If NO / not for them:**

> "Understood — not every portfolio has the same profile. Can I ask: is it the model that doesn't fit, or is the timing off right now?"

**[If timing:]** > "No problem — when would be a better time to revisit? I'll put a note in the diary."

**[If model:]** > "Fair enough. Is there anyone in your network who owns commercial assets who might have a different situation? We have a partner referral programme — 2% of everything we earn from any client you introduce, no cap."
