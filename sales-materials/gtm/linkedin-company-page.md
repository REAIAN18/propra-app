### Post 2 — FL Market Insight (Educational, no pitch)

```
Florida commercial insurance is expensive. Here's how expensive.

A 45,000 sqft office in Coral Gables should carry an all-risk premium of around $84,000/year at current market rates. We routinely see the same asset at $112,000/year — a $28,000 overpay — because the placement was made eight years ago and never retendered.

Three reasons this happens:

1. Legacy single-carrier placement. One broker, one insurer, no competition at renewal.
2. Rebuilding cost schedules not updated post-2021. Inflation meant insurers revalued mid-term and spiked premiums. Most owners didn't notice.
3. Windstorm carved out separately, with primary and Citizens policies not optimised together.

Industry data: commercial insurance retender saves 20–35% on premium in Florida.
(Lockton Real Estate Practice; Gallagher Bassett FL Market Update Q4 2024)

If you haven't retendered your commercial book in the last two years, you're probably overpaying. The number is specific to your assets, your sqft, your loss runs — not a round estimate.

Worth knowing where you stand.
```
