## POST 4 — Thursday: Rent Roll Problem
*Hook: specific mechanism most owners don't think about.*

---

Here's a thing that costs Florida commercial landlords more than almost anything else, quietly:

Letting a rent review pass without preparing for it.

Most leases have rent review clauses — typically every 3–5 years. At review date, you put forward a new rent. The tenant counters. You negotiate.

The outcome depends almost entirely on how well you've documented comparable rents in the 6–12 months before the review date.

Most owners don't track comps. The review arrives, their attorney quotes a rate from whatever's to hand, and they settle for less than the market would support.

On a 10-asset portfolio, the cumulative cost of underprepared rent reviews over one lease cycle is often $30k–$60k in foregone income. Gone. Permanently.

RealHQ maps every lease against live market comparables. We build the case before the review date, not at it.

This is one of five things we do. Commission-only. No cost unless we move the number.
