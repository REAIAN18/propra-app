Most commercial leases have a rent review clause. Most landlords underuse it.

Here's how rent review works — and why timing matters more than most people realise.

In a standard lease, rent is fixed at the start. Every 3–5 years, you have a window to reset it to the open market rent. You put forward a number. The tenant counters. You negotiate.

The outcome is almost entirely determined by what happened in the 6–12 months before the review date.

If you've been tracking comparable rents — similar units, similar locations, recent lettings — you have leverage. You can defend a higher figure with evidence. The tenant's surveyor can't dismiss it.

If you haven't, you're negotiating from memory. The tenant's surveyor is not.

On a 10-asset portfolio, the cumulative gap between a well-prepared review and a poorly-prepared one is typically £30,000–£60,000 over one lease cycle.

That's permanent income loss. The rent you don't recover at review doesn't come back.
