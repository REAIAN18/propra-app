"My broker handles everything."

Three reasons this might be costing you.

1. Your broker earns a commission from the insurer — not from finding you the lowest premium. A higher premium means a higher commission. The incentive is not aligned with yours.

2. Brokers place and renew. They don't audit what they placed two years ago against what the market is doing today. That work falls to no one.

3. "Handles everything" usually means handles renewal. It doesn't mean benchmarks your rate against 14 competing carriers, or flags that your rebuilding cost schedule is still based on 2021 valuations.

The broker isn't the problem. The assumption that placement equals ongoing optimisation is.

Your portfolio needs someone whose commission is a percentage of what they save you — not what they place.
