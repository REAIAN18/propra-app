## Selection Rationale (SE UK)

Target profile: SE England commercial landlords with 3–20 assets. Primary focus:
- **M25 corridor industrial/logistics** — highest rent growth market in UK, most leases at historic ERV
- **Home Counties office parks** — insurance cost inflation + occupancy pressure post-COVID
- **Kent/Surrey/Essex mixed-use retail** — constrained supply, rent reviews missed

Pain-point lead logic:
- **Industrial/logistics** → Energy first (large sq-ft, 24hr operations, often on default rollover tariffs post-contract expiry), then rent review (ERV up 30–40% in 3 years)
- **Office** → Insurance (commercial property insurance up 25–40% in SE since 2020, rarely re-tendered) + energy
- **Retail/mixed-use** → Rent review (many retail leases at pre-2021 passing rent, well below current ERV), then insurance
- **Active acquirers** → Full operating cost audit on inherited contracts

## Market context notes (SE UK)

- **Energy**: UK commercial gas/electricity prices peaked Q4 2022. Default tariffs post-expiry are still elevated. Most landlords renewed or rolled at peak; switching to 2024-competitive contracts typically saves 15–25% vs current contracted rate.
- **Insurance**: Lloyds/London market commercial property rates rose 2019–2022. Portfolio consolidation typically delivers 20–30% saving vs disaggregated placement.
- **Industrial rents**: CBRE/Savills data shows M25 industrial ERV growth of 35–50% in 2021–2024. Most pre-2022 leases are materially below market.
- **Retail**: Town centre recovery markets (Canterbury, Guildford, Tunbridge Wells) showing 15–25% rental growth since 2021 trough.
