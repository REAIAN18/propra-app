> **FL context:** Florida remains one of the most expensive commercial insurance markets in the US. Post-Ian/Irma loss years drove carrier exits (Bankers Insurance, several Lloyd's syndicates) — market tightened 2022–2024. As of 2025 there are signs of modest softening for industrial inland, but coastal retail and office are still 15–25% above national averages.

### Why FL Owners Overpay
- **Legacy single-carrier placement** — broker placed 10 years ago, never retendered. Common with regional CRE managers.
- **Poor loss runs presentation** — one historic water damage claim inflates subsequent quotes if not contextualized.
- **Rebuilding cost estimates not updated** — inflation 2021–2023 means most schedules are understated. When insurers revalue mid-term, premiums spike.
- **Windstorm carved out separately** — owners often carry both a primary policy + Citizens windstorm without optimizing the combined structure.
- **No co-insurance coordination** — tenants' content and landlord fabric policies overlap, paying twice for the same risk.

### Average Savings on Retender
- **Typical saving: 20–35% on premium at retender** (CoStar/CBRE Facility Cost Index 2024, Lockton RE survey)
- Dollar terms: a 45,000 sqft office paying $112k/yr → market equivalent ~$84k → **$28k annual saving** (matches Coral Gables demo asset)
- Industrial: $42k actual vs $28k market = **$14k/yr leakage** (matches Tampa Industrial demo asset)
- Retail (Brickell coastal): $68k vs $48k market = **$20k/yr** (coastal loading + lack of competition)

## 2. Energy Benchmarks — FL Commercial

### Average Electricity Rate
- **FPL (Florida Power & Light) commercial rate: 9.8–11.2 ¢/kWh** (2025 average including demand charges; FPSC filings)
- **Duke Energy Florida: 10.1–11.8 ¢/kWh** (commercial, tiered by demand; FPSC 2025)
- **Tampa Electric (TECO): 9.5–11.0 ¢/kWh**
- National commercial average: ~12.0 ¢/kWh — FL is slightly below average for generation, but demand charges and HVAC intensity drive total bills up

### Typical Energy Intensity by Asset Type (FL climate, kWh/sqft/yr)
| Asset Type | National Benchmark | FL Adjusted | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Office | 18–22 kWh/sqft | 22–28 kWh/sqft | +25–35% vs national — HVAC load in FL heat/humidity |
| Retail | 20–30 kWh/sqft | 24–34 kWh/sqft | AC-heavy; lighting intensive |
| Industrial / Warehouse | 6–10 kWh/sqft | 8–12 kWh/sqft | Varies hugely by process use |
| Flex | 12–18 kWh/sqft | 15–22 kWh/sqft | Mixed use drives range |

> **Demo alignment:** Tampa Industrial (28,000 sqft) at $84k actual energy cost = $3.00/sqft; market benchmark $2.00/sqft. The gap ($28k/yr) is consistent with a building on a legacy utility tariff without demand management.

### Solar Payback in FL
- FL ranks **3rd nationally for solar irradiance** (avg 5.5–5.8 peak sun hours/day; NREL data)
- **Commercial rooftop solar payback: 5–7 years** for systems installed 2024–2025 (post-IRA credits)
- ITC (Investment Tax Credit): **30% federal credit** under Inflation Reduction Act (through 2032)
- Typical commercial system: $2.00–$2.50/W installed (utility-scale economics trickling into commercial)
- 250 kWp system (Tampa Industrial rooftop) → ~350 MWh/yr → **~$35k–45k/yr electricity offset or export income**
- Duke/FPL net metering: commercial net metering available; check specific tariff (FL PSC Order 2022)

### EV Charging Income Potential
- **Level 2 commercial charger (J1772): $1,800–$3,200/bay/year** net revenue (after electricity cost and management fee)
- **DC Fast Charger: $8,000–$18,000/bay/year** (high utilisation urban locations; EVgo/ChargePoint operator data 2024)
- Demo portfolio uses conservative Level 2 estimate: **$2,400/bay/year** — well within credible range
- EV penetration in FL: 4.2% of registered vehicles (2025); Miami-Dade and Broward lead the state
- Key citation: BloombergNEF EV Charging Economics Report 2024; ChargePoint Fleet Data 2025

### Energy Savings Opportunity
- Switching from legacy tariff to time-of-use + demand management: **10–18% bill reduction**
- LED lighting retrofit (if not done): **15–25% reduction** on lighting load
- HVAC controls upgrade (BMS): **12–20% HVAC cost saving** — massive in FL given cooling dominance
- Combined: **25–40% energy cost reduction** feasible for assets still on legacy arrangements

## 4. Compliance — FL Commercial

### Key Certificates Required
| Certificate | Asset Types | Renewal Cycle | Issuing Body |
|---|---|---|---|
| Certificate of Occupancy (CO) | All | On material change / renovation | Local building dept |
| Fire Safety Inspection | All | Annual | Local Fire Marshal |
| Elevator/Escalator Permit | Multi-story buildings | Annual | FL DBPR (Dept of Business & Professional Regulation) |
| Electrical Installation Certificate (EICR equivalent) | All (FL: NFPA 70 compliance) | Every 5 years or on change of use | Licensed FL electrician + AHJ sign-off |
| HVAC Operational Permit | All commercial | Annual for some jurisdictions | Local building dept |
| Environmental (Phase I ESA) | Industrial, gas stations, dry cleaners | Every 3–5 years if site conditions change | FL DEP / lender requirement |
| Health Inspection (tenant-facing) | Retail food service | Annual + periodic unannounced | FL Dept of Agriculture / local |
| Windstorm Mitigation Inspection | Coastal / wind-exposed | At sale, refinancing, or insurer request | FL-licensed inspector |

### Common Fines for Non-Compliance (FL)
- **Fire Safety violation:** $250–$500/day per violation; can escalate to $15,000+ for systemic failure (FL NFPA local ordinances)
- **Elevator without valid permit:** $500–$2,000/day; potential closure order (DBPR Ch. 399 FS)
- **Electrical non-compliance:** $1,000–$25,000 per inspection cycle if flagged by AHJ
- **Environmental (Phase I expired, site change):** FDEP enforcement $10,000–$50,000+ if contamination event occurs without baseline
- **Unpermitted work discovered:** double permit fee + potential stop-work order; affects CO validity

### Certificate Renewal Cycles — Quick Reference
- **Annual:** Fire safety, elevator permits, HVAC (some jurisdictions), health inspections (food)
- **Every 3 years:** Sprinkler systems full flow test
- **Every 5 years:** Full EICR equivalent (NFPA 70 inspection); Phase I ESA (lender-triggered)
- **At sale / refi:** Windstorm mitigation, Phase I/II ESA, CO confirmation

## 5. Financing — FL CRE

### Current Rate Environment (Q1 2026)
- **SOFR (30-day):** ~4.30% (Fed holding; cut cycle slower than 2024 forwards implied)
- **Typical SOFR + spread for FL CRE:**
  - Industrial / stabilised: **SOFR + 175–225 bps** → all-in ~6.05–6.55%
  - Office (higher risk): **SOFR + 250–325 bps** → all-in ~6.80–7.55%
  - Retail (strong-tenanted): **SOFR + 200–275 bps** → all-in ~6.30–7.05%
  - Construction / value-add: **SOFR + 300–400 bps** → all-in 7.30–8.30%
- **Fixed rate (life co / CMBS):** 10-yr Treasury (~4.20%) + 150–225 bps → **5.70–6.45%** for stabilised
